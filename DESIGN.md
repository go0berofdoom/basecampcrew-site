# BaseCamp visual direction study

## 1. Trail Ledger

- Background / accent: parchment `#F2EBDD`, deep green `#1D6F42`, signal orange `#E66A2C`
- Type: Georgia Display (headlines) + ui-monospace (labels and navigational detail)
- Art style: screen-printed park maps, contour lines, stamped labels, spare ink textures
- Rationale: Feels useful and outdoors-rooted, like a beautifully kept trip log rather than a conventional software landing page.

## 2. Night Signal

- Background / accent: ink navy `#081B20`, lantern amber `#F5B942`, alpine green `#2C8B59`
- Type: Palatino (headlines) + Avenir Next (body)
- Art style: moonlit cut-paper landscapes, luminous route lines, campsite windows glowing in the dark
- Rationale: Premium and cinematic, with the app framed as the light that guides a crew through fragmented planning.

## 3. Field Station

- Background / accent: warm canvas `#F4F0E6`, forest `#195B39`, survey red `#C64D35`
- Type: Rockwell (headlines) + Gill Sans (body)
- Art style: national-park field guides, diagrammatic RV cutaways, numbered specimens and botanical silhouettes
- Rationale: Communicates trusted expertise and makes the 2,000+ resort catalog feel curated instead of merely large.

## 4. Modern Trailhead — selected

- Background / accent: mist `#F3F5EE`, BaseCamp green `#1D6F42`, campfire `#FF7A3D`; dark mode uses midnight pine `#071C15`
- Type: Charter / Georgia (expressive editorial headlines) + Avenir Next / Trebuchet MS (clean humanist body) + ui-monospace (utility labels)
- Art style: bold editorial landscapes built from crisp SVG layers, trail-map arcs, locator pins, friendly RV silhouettes, and a dramatic phone-call beacon
- Rationale: The strongest balance of outdoorsy warmth, premium restraint, and modern product clarity. It preserves the deep-green brand anchor, gives the flagship phone-calling feature a memorable visual language, and avoids both rustic nostalgia and generic SaaS polish.

### Direction decision

Modern Trailhead is selected. Its editorial typography and tactile trail-map illustration make the experience distinctly recreational, while the restrained palette, spacious composition, and crisp interface fragments make BaseCamp feel capable enough to coordinate real-world logistics. Campfire orange is reserved for moments of action—availability found, a call connected, or a site opening—so the visual system reinforces the product story.

### Visual rules for the build

1. Use one dominant illustration per major section, drawn inline as accessible SVG where it conveys meaning and hidden from assistive technology when decorative.
2. Keep forest green structural; reserve orange for active signals and small highlights.
3. Use contour lines, coordinate labels, dots, and dashed routes as a recurring connective motif.
4. Avoid device mockup clichés. Product capabilities appear as tangible campsite objects, maps, message notes, and call signals.
5. Motion should feel like arriving at camp: slow vertical reveals, gentle drift, and route drawing—never bouncy or distracting.
